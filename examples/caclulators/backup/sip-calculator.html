What is a SIP?

SIP or Systematic Investment Plan, by definition, is a mode of investment offered by mutual funds, wherein you can invest a fixed amount of money at regular intervals - this could be monthly or quarterly. Some investment experts draw a parallel between SIPs and Recurring Deposit - the only difference is that, in the latter the money is invested in a mutual fund scheme.

With its structured approach, SIP brings balance and discipline to the investment, and is a great tool for wealth creation. Since the investor invests in a time-bound manner, he/she does not have to worry about the market dynamics. It is the best way to achieve long-term goals including retirement planning, buying a house or children's higher education. SIPs have an added advantage of the power of compounding investments. The longer you stay invested, the more you gain.

Benefits of SIP

There are several benefits of investing in a SIP. Let's have a look:

  • Small investments: The best part of SIPs is that you can invest small amounts of money, even if it's only ₹500.
  • Rupee-cost averaging: The rupee-cost averaging is a big reason why investors go for SIPs. It helps you buy more units when the price is lower and fewer units when the price is higher. In addition, it also helps to dilute the effect of short-term market fluctuation on your investments, making it beneficial in the long-term.
  • Power of compounding: The money that you invest every month or over a certain period gets compounded over time through regular investments. If you start early, you can see its effect even more!
  • Instills discipline: SIP is modeled in such a way that it encourages discipline and regularity in investments.

How to use the SIP Calculator

The online SIP calculator makes your task simpler and lets you calculate the amount you need to start investing or the amount you will receive at the end of the investment period. That's not all - you can also modify the details to check how it makes a difference to your overall returns.

Let's understand how the calculator functions based on your requirement:

1. You want to invest a fixed amount monthly and want to know the future value of the investment.

  • Step 1: Select 'I want to invest'
  • Step 2: Select the amount you want to invest monthly
  • Step 3: Enter the investment period you have decided to invest for and the expected rate of return.
  • Step 4: The SIP calculator will then tell you how much you will receive at the end of your investment period, your total investment and capital gains.

2. You are investing for a goal and want to know the required monthly investment to meet the goal

  • Step 1: Select 'I know my Goal'
  • Step 2: Enter the amount you want to save for your goal.
  • Step 3: Enter the horizon of your goal in years and percentage of expected return.
  • Step 4: Basis the details you provide, the calculator will tell you how much you should start investing monthly towards a SIP.

FAQs

Are SIPs tax free?

No, not at all SIP plans are tax-free. only the SIPs in ELSS Mutual Funds are eligible for tax deduction of up to ₹1.5 lakh per annum under Section 80C.

What is the interest rate on SIPs?

SIP returns (or interest rate) vary a lot depending upon the asset class chosen. A SIP in equity scheme yields higher returns than the one in Debt schemes. On an average, for SIP in large cap equity funds, a return of 12-15% can be expected whereas from mid-cap equities, a return of 14-17% can be expected.

Which is better RD or SIP?

There is no one correct answer for this question. It depends on the investor's investment objective & risk capacity. Since SIPs are market linked, the upside on returns are considerably higher as compared to FD which is a fixed income product. For aggressive investors, SIP in Equity Funds or ELSS is best suited. Even for conservative investors looking to limit short term risk, a SIP in a liquid or Debt funds can generate higher returns than Recurring Deposits at negligible risks.

Can we withdraw SIP anytime?

Yes, you can withdraw money from SIPs anytime you wish, except for Equity Linked Savings Scheme (ELSS) which has a lock-in period of 3 years.

What if I miss an installment on a SIP date or don't have sufficient money in my bank account?

Your SIP will continue uninterrupted and you can continue paying SIP installments from the next due date. There is no penalty on skipping or missing a SIP installment, however to maximize your returns, it is recommended to pay every SIP installment on it's due date.

Can I increase the SIP Amount?

Yes, you can. While taking a new SIP, you can opt for a periodic top-up of your investment amount. If you did not opt for auto top while registering your SIP, you can always start a new SIP in the same scheme, the units allotted in the new SIP, wll be added to your portfolio.
